What each number means (and why it matters for search)

1Size — dimensions & total voxels

The bounding box X × Y × Z; total voxels is their product. A 1000³ map is a billion cells — pure scale, before any obstacle. It sets memory and runtime, and lets you compare "big vs small" maps.

▶ For search: caps how large OPEN/CLOSED can grow; the first thing that decides whether a map even fits in memory.

2Free fraction (porosity) — free / total

What share of cells are passable. Near 1.0 = wide open (Warframe debris in lots of empty space); low = packed with obstacles (Sandstone rock).

▶ For search: high free fraction → the octile heuristic is nearly perfect (few detours); low → many obstacles bend paths and the heuristic loses grip — exactly the obstacle regime in your INC-vs-AGG work.

3Components & unreachable fraction

Group the free cells into connected components (islands you can travel within). The biggest one is the real navigable space; everything else — sealed pockets, holes inside rock — is unreachable. unreachable_fraction = (free − largest_component) / free.

▶ For search: tells you how much of the "free" space is a trap. A high value (porous Sandstone) means many start/goal pairs are simply unsolvable; a near-zero value (open Warframe) means one big blob.

Efficient algorithm: one connected-components labelling pass (union-find / scipy.ndimage.label with 26-connectivity) gives every component and its size in O(N). Then unreachable = free − size(largest). No per-query BFS needed.

4Clearance (openness) — distance from each free cell to the nearest obstacle

A distance map: small near walls, large in the middle of open volume. Mean clearance = how roomy the map is on average; max clearance = the radius of its biggest cavity. Tight pipes (Industrial Plants) score low; open arenas score high.

▶ For search: low clearance ⇒ narrow corridors ⇒ few real move choices per node (low effective branching) and lots of forced moves — where jump-point-style shortcuts pay off most.

5Detour ratio (difficulty) — optimal_cost / heuristic(start, goal)

The straight-line octile distance is a lower bound; the real shortest path is usually longer because obstacles force detours. Ratio = 1.0 means the heuristic was exact (clear shot); > 1.0 means obstacles made it work. Averaged over a map's problem set, this is the single best one-number measure of how hard the map is.

▶ For search: this is heuristic accuracy. It comes free from the scenario files (they store optimal and heuristic distance per instance) and is the most research-relevant column for heuristic-search experiments.

Cluster the maps with these 5. Scale (1), porosity (2), fragmentation (3), openness (4), and hardness (5) are near-independent axes — plot or k-means on them and the four domains (Warframe, Sandstone, Descent, Industrial Plants) should separate cleanly.
