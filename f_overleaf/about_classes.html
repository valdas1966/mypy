Document Classes

The argument inside \documentclass{...} — what type of document you're building

Standard Classes

article MOST COMMON
Papers, short documents
The go-to class for academic papers, homework, short reports, and documentation. No chapters — uses sections as the top-level heading.
sections no chapters single-sided abstract
report
Long documents, theses
Like article but with chapters. Good for theses, technical reports, and longer structured documents. Title page on its own page.
chapters sections single-sided abstract
book
Full books
Designed for full books with parts, chapters, front/back matter. Two-sided by default (different left/right margins for binding).
parts chapters two-sided no abstract
letter
Formal letters
For writing formal letters with sender/recipient addresses, date, signature, and enclosures. Very specific layout commands.
address signature no sections no abstract

Popular Extended Classes

beamer PRESENTATIONS
Slides, presentations
Creates presentation slides with frames, transitions, and themes. The LaTeX alternative to PowerPoint. Supports overlays and animations.
frames themes overlays handouts
standalone
Single figures, TikZ
Minimal class that crops the page to fit the content. Perfect for creating standalone figures, diagrams, or TikZ pictures for inclusion elsewhere.
auto-crop minimal no pages
memoir
Flexible, all-purpose
A powerful class that combines features of article, report, and book. Highly customizable with many built-in tools for typography and layout.
chapters sections customizable all-in-one
IEEEtran
IEEE journal papers
Formatted specifically for IEEE journal and conference submissions. Two-column layout, specific citation style, and IEEE-compliant formatting.
two-column IEEE format bibliography
