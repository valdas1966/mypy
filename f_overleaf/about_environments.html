Environments

\begin{...} ... \end{...} — blocks that apply specific formatting rules

What Is an Environment?

An environment is a block of content wrapped in \begin{name} and \end{name}. It applies specific formatting or behavior to everything inside it.

The document environment is special — it's the only mandatory environment. Everything visible in your PDF must be inside it.

Document Structure

\documentclass{article}
\usepackage{...}
PREAMBLE — settings, not visible
↓
\begin{document}
Your content goes here
(text, sections, figures, math, ...)
\end{document}
Anything after \end{document} is ignored

Common Environments

Text & Lists
itemize
Bullet (unordered) list
\begin{itemize}
  \item First point
  \item Second point
\end{itemize}
enumerate
Numbered (ordered) list
\begin{enumerate}
  \item Step one
  \item Step two
\end{enumerate}
abstract
Paper abstract section
\begin{abstract}
  This paper presents...
\end{abstract}
verbatim
Literal text (no formatting)
\begin{verbatim}
  Code or text as-is
  \commands are NOT parsed
\end{verbatim}
Math
equation
Numbered equation (centered)
\begin{equation}
  E = mc^2
\end{equation}
align
Multiple aligned equations
\begin{align}
  x &= 2y + 3 \\
  y &= z - 1
\end{align}
Figures & Tables
figure
Floating figure with caption
\begin{figure}[h]
  \includegraphics{img.png}
  \caption{My figure}
\end{figure}
table + tabular
Table with rows and columns
\begin{table}[h]
  \begin{tabular}{|l|r|}
    Name & Score \\ \hline
    Alice & 95 \\
  \end{tabular}
\end{table}

Key Concept

Environments can be nested

You can put environments inside other environments — a tabular inside a table, an itemize inside an enumerate, or a figure inside a document. The only rule: every \begin must have a matching \end.
