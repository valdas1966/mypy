▍ The idea in one breath

My default voice is whatever feels natural for the answer — sometimes that runs long. Rather than forcing one fixed style on every reply, skills act as focusing lenses you snap on in the moment. /tldr is the “make it short” lens: it takes the reply I just gave and re-states it tight — leading with the answer, cutting the padding, keeping every fact.

▍ A classic example

You asked: “How do I rename a file in Python?”

DEFAULT REPLY~120 words

Great question! There are a few ways to rename a file in Python, depending on your needs and which module you prefer to use…

The classic approach uses the os module. You'd call os.rename(src, dst). Note that the file must exist, the destination directory must exist, and on some platforms an existing destination behaves differently…

Alternatively, the more modern pathlib gives you Path('old').rename('new'), which many consider cleaner. You may also want a try/except around it to handle FileNotFoundError…

AFTER /tldr2 lines

os.rename('old.txt', 'new.txt')

Or, modern style: Path('old.txt').rename('new.txt').
